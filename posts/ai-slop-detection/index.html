Detecting AI slop writing

Living reference. Minimal rules for me and my agents.

Rules

  1. No em dashes.
  2. No "It's not X, it's Y."
  3. No "curious about".
  4. No "genuinely".
  5. No forced-casual slang ("absolutely slaps"). Reads like a 2017 millennial stereotype.
  6. No overly positive tone.
  7. No writing that is too clean for the context. Internet comments are not essays.
  8. No AI vocabulary: delve, leverage, navigate, elevate, intricate, meticulously, synergy, empower, landscape, ecosystem, underscore, seamless, robust, game-changer.
  9. No ad-speak: boasts, vibrant, nestled, in the heart of, renowned, groundbreaking, committed to, showcasing, a diverse array of.
  10. No fake self-answering questions ("What does this mean? Faster results.").
  11. No connector words on every sentence: Additionally, Furthermore, Moreover, However, Importantly.
  12. No drama or bait: "This changes everything", "paradigm shift", "We're witnessing history".
  13. No Headings With Every Word Capitalized.
  14. No forced lists inside paragraphs ("First... Second... Third...") when the idea doesn't need it.
  15. No name-dropping press or brands as social proof.
  16. No too-perfect punctuation: curly quotes, proper ellipses, en dashes in ranges. Humans type fast: straight quotes, "...", "--".
  17. No parallel-structure stat ledes ("X became Y. X became Y.").
  18. No sentence fragments ("Zero framework. Plain HTML on GitHub Pages."). Always full sentences, except in bullet points.
